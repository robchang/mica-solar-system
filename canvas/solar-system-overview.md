# The Solar System

## Overview

The Solar System is our cosmic neighborhood — a gravitationally bound system consisting of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It formed 4.6 billion years ago from a giant molecular cloud of gas and dust.

## The Sun

At the center lies the Sun, a G-type main-sequence star containing 99.8% of the Solar System's total mass. It generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium at its core, and provides the light and heat that make life on Earth possible.

## The Planets

Eight planets orbit the Sun, divided into two groups:

### Inner Planets (Terrestrial)

1. **Mercury** — The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, extreme temperature swings (from -180°C to 430°C), and a heavily cratered surface. Orbital period: 88 days.

2. **Venus** — Often called Earth's "sister planet" due to similar size. It has a thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide with sulfuric acid clouds, creating a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet (~465°C). It rotates backwards compared to most planets.

3. **Earth** — Our home, the only known planet with life. It has liquid water on its surface, a protective magnetic field, and a single natural satellite (the Moon). Orbital period: 365.25 days.

4. **Mars** — The "Red Planet" due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has the tallest volcano (Olympus Mons) and the deepest canyon (Valles Marineris) in the Solar System. Evidence suggests it once had liquid water. Orbital period: 687 days.

### Outer Planets (Gas Giants & Ice Giants)

5. **Jupiter** — The largest planet, with a mass more than twice that of all other planets combined. Its Great Red Spot is a giant storm larger than Earth. It has at least 95 known moons, including the four large Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. Orbital period: ~12 years.

6. **Saturn** — Famous for its spectacular ring system made of ice and rock particles. It is the least dense planet — it would float in water if there were a bathtub big enough. It has 146 known moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere. Orbital period: ~29 years.

7. **Uranus** — An ice giant that rotates on its side (axial tilt of ~98°). It has a blue-green color from methane in its atmosphere. Discovered in 1781 by William Herschel. Orbital period: ~84 years.

8. **Neptune** — The most distant planet, with the strongest winds in the Solar System (up to 2,100 km/h). It has a vivid blue color and at least 16 known moons, the largest being Triton. Orbital period: ~165 years.

## Dwarf Planets

- **Pluto** — Once considered the ninth planet, reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. It has five known moons, including Charon, which is so large relative to Pluto that they orbit a common center of gravity.
- Other recognized dwarf planets include Ceres, Eris, Haumea, and Makemake.

## The Asteroid Belt

Between Mars and Jupiter lies the Asteroid Belt, a region containing millions of rocky remnants from the Solar System's formation. The largest object, Ceres, is also a dwarf planet.

## The Kuiper Belt and Beyond

Beyond Neptune, the Kuiper Belt is a disk-shaped region populated by icy bodies, including Pluto and many other dwarf planets. Further out lies the Oort Cloud, a theoretical spherical shell of trillions of comets marking the edge of the Sun's gravitational influence — roughly one light-year away.

## Scale

The Solar System spans roughly 2 light-hours to 2 light-years in diameter, depending on where you draw the boundary. The distance from the Sun to Earth is about 150 million kilometers (1 Astronomical Unit, or AU). Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes to reach Earth.

## Key Facts

- **Age:** ~4.6 billion years
- **Total known planets:** 8
- **Total known dwarf planets:** 5+
- **Total known moons:** 290+
- **Heliopause distance:** ~120 AU (Voyager 1 crossed it in 2012)
- **Nearest star system:** Proxima Centauri, 4.24 light-years away
